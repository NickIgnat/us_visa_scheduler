<div><h1>General Required Documents - For All Visa Types</h1>
<p>The following documents are required for all visa types:</p>
<ol>
<li>Current Passport valid for travel to the United States. The passport must be valid for at least six months beyond the period of stay in the United States (unless exempt by <a href="https://www.cbp.gov/document/bulletins/six-month-club-update" target="_blank">country-specific agreements</a>.)</li>
<li>Passport containing the most recently issued U.S. visa (if applicable).</li>
<li>Nonimmigrant Visa Application, Form DS-160 confirmation page.</li>
<li>Confirmation and Instructions page printed from this website</li>
<li>One 5 x 5 cm (2 x 2 inches) color photo taken within the last 6 months. Further details regarding Department of State photo guidelines can be found at: <a href="https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/photos.html" target="_blank">https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/photos.html</a>.</li>
<li>Accompanying family members, unless entering the United States for another purpose, should present a marriage certificate (spouse) and/or birth certificate (for unmarried children under 21), as applicable.</li>
<li>Applicants who have a medical ineligibility or who have been denied entry, deported or overstayed must provide additional documents at the time of their visa interview. <a href="https://uk.usembassy.gov/visas/ineligibilities-and-waivers-2/" target="_blank">Click on this link for a complete list of additional required documents to bring to the interview.</a></li>
<br>
<p><strong>PLEASE NOTE:</strong></p>
<p><strong>If you have EVER been arrested, convicted, or cautioned you are required to furnish a police certificate from the ACPO Criminal Records Office. The Rehabilitation of Offenders Act does NOT apply to U.S. visa law and a spent conviction must be declared. Failure to present a police certificate at the time of your interview will delay the processing of your application.</strong> <a href="https://uk.usembassy.gov/visas/ineligibilities-and-waivers-2/arrest-caution-conviction/" target="_blank">Click on this link for the complete list of additional required documents to bring to the interview.</a></p>
</ol>
<p>If you are attending an appointment in person, you must bring original documents. If you are eligible to send your documents via courier, you must send your current valid passport and the prior passport containing the most recently issued U.S visa (if applicable), as well as the DS-160 confirmation page and copies of any other supporting documents. Please see the section below for a list of supporting documents required for each visa type.</p></div>
<div><h2>(B) VISITOR: BUSINESS, TOURISM, MEDICAL TREATMENT</h2>
<ul>
<li>Be ready to present the following documents, if traveling for medical treatment:</li>
<ul>
<li>Medical diagnosis from a local physician, explaining the nature of the ailment and the reason treatment is needed in the United States.</li>
<li>Letter from a physician or medical facility in the United States stating the willingness to treat the diagnosed ailment, and detailing the projected length and cost of treatment (including doctors' fees, hospitalization fees, and all medical-related expenses).</li>
<li>Proof that the patient's transportation, medical, and living expenses in the United States will be paid. This may be in the form of bank or other statements of income/savings or certified copies of income tax returns (either the patient's or the person or organization paying for the treatment).</li>
</ul>
<li>You may apply for a B-1 visitor visa to work temporarily in the U.S. as a personal or domestic employee in limited situations. You and your employer will be required to prove the employment relationship meets the wages and working conditions of the U.S. For further details on how to apply, visit your local U.S. Embassy’s website and <a href="https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/tourism-visit/visitor.html" target="_blank">https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/tourism-visit/visitor.html</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>When attending an in-person consular interview, additional documents may be requested to establish if you are qualified for the visa. If you qualify for NOT attending an in-person consular interview, submit only the required documents specifically listed on the instruction page.</p>
<p>For example, additional requested documents for an in-person interview may include evidence of:</p>
<ul>
<li>The purpose of your trip</li>
<li>Your intent to depart the United States after your trip; and/or</li>
<li>Your ability to pay all the costs of your trip.</li>
<li>Evidence of your employment and/or family ties may be sufficient to show the purpose of your trip and your intent to return to your home country.</li>
<li>If you cannot cover all the costs for your trip, you may show evidence that another person will cover some or all of the costs for your trip.</li>
</ul></div>
</p>
